d-code — Mission & North Star

Mission

Empower blind, low-vision, deaf, hard-of-hearing, and sighted learners to master braille keyboarding and foundational sign language—quickly, confidently, and for free—through playful, accessible lessons that work on any device and in any classroom.

Why it matters

  • Braille literacy opens doors to education, employment, and independence.
  • Keyboard fluency accelerates screen-reader use and digital participation.
  • Sign language basics build bridges between deaf/hard-of-hearing and hearing communities.
  • Inclusive tools benefit everyone—teachers, families, and peers.

Who we serve

  • Primary: Blind and low-vision learners beginning braille or strengthening touch typing; deaf and hard-of-hearing learners exploring sign language.
  • Secondary: Educators, tutors, parents, and allies who need ready-to-use materials.
  • Inclusive: Sighted and hearing classmates learning braille and sign basics to build inclusive communities.

How we do it (pillars)

  1. Access First — Works with screen readers, captions, large text, high contrast, and offline-friendly modules.
  2. Learn by Doing — Short drills, games, and real-world tasks (notes, chat, search, forms, greetings).
  3. Local & Multilingual — English and Tagalog at launch; open to community translations.
  4. Data-Light — Optimized for low bandwidth and shared devices.
  5. Teacher-Ready — Printable guides, lesson plans, and progress tracking.
  6. Open & Evolving — Open content where possible; invites feedback and contributions.

Commitments

  • WCAG AA+ accessibility as a baseline; test with real users.
  • No paywalls for core learning paths.
  • Privacy-first analytics and transparent data practices.

What success looks like

  • Learners complete a beginner braille path in under 2 hours with 90%+ accuracy.
  • Learners master sign alphabet and 20+ everyday signs within a week.
  • Weekly active learners grow via word-of-mouth in schools and orgs.
  • Educators adopt d-code materials in lesson plans and community trainings.
  • Community contributors localize content and add drills.

Voice & tone

Warm, clear, encouraging. We replace jargon with plain language and celebrate every small win.
